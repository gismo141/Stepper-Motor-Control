5 ## Overview
6 
7 This project is an example for a system-on-a-chip-design of a stepper-motor-control using the NIOS II/s Processor on the development boards from [terasIC](http://www.terasic.com.tw/cgi-bin/page/archive.pl?Language=English&CategoryNo=163#Category165).

11 ## Software
12 
13 The following software is beeing used for the development:
14 
15 - VHDL-Simulation: [ModelSim - Starter Edition (14.0.2)](http://www.altera.com/products/software/quartus-ii/modelsim/qts-modelsim-index.html)
16 - VHDL-Source- and Project-Management: [Quartus II - Web Edition (14.0.0)](http://www.altera.com/products/software/quartus-ii/web-edition/qts-we-index.html)
17 - C-Source-Management: [NIOS (based on Eclipse, integrated in Quartus)](http://www.altera.com/devices/processor/nios2/tools/ide/ni2-ide.html)

19 ## Development
20 
21 The development is devided into 4 so-called *milestones*:
22 
23 ##### 1. Basic Processor Development
24 
25 [a) Design-Phase](Documentation/Milestone_1a/Meilenstein_1a.md):
26  - UML-diagrams;
27  - project-planning;
28  - tasks-overview;
29  - interrupt-service-routines;
30  - listing of OS-components (events, mutexes, semaphores, message queues, etc).
31 
32 b) NIOS II/s Base-Software without stepper-component:
33  - Proof-of-Concept with working C-Sources
34  - complete Doxygen-documentation
35 
36 ##### 2. NIOS II/s Base-Software with register-interface
37 
38 - presenting the solution
39  + show control-status (`ctrlReg`) on red-LEDs ([chapter 8.2.4]())
40  + show speed-status (`speedReg`) on green-LEDs ([chapter 8.2.4]())
41  + show steps-status (`stepsReg`) on NIOS-console (with changing values in debug-mode)
42  + testing of the steppermotor-ISR by setting the `requestbit` every 4s
43 - development of VHDL-register-interface
44  + listing and description of chosen desing-parameters
45  + block-diagram of the register-interface showing all signals
46  + Proof-of-Concept with working VHDL-Sources
47  + available simulation-files
48 
49 ##### 3. Motor-Control-Unit
50 
51 a) Idea of Design
52  - block-diagram for every component
53  - state-diagrams (if necessary)
54  - textual representation of the design-idea
55 
56 b) Isolated Control via DE0 / DE2 - Board-Switches
57  - presenting the solution
58  + metering of the important speedsteps 0, 1 and 7
59  + qualitative presentation of every other speedsteps
60  + demonstration of every usage-mode
61  - Proof-of-Concept with working VHDL-Sources
62  - available simulation-files
63 
64 ##### 4. Combined Solution
65 
66 combining the motor-control-unit with the NIOS II/s Base-Software with register-interface
